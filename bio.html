Bio

My name is Rowan Chad Muad'Dib Valle y Carmenatty, however I just go by Rowan because although I love the traditional Hispanic naming conventions, that's too verbose, and I like mononyms.

I joined the army at 18 and went to Iraq soon after. I was deployed in "the sandbox" for 15 months where I performed patrol and QRF (quick reactionary force) duties, and also helped secure and guard territory and engaged the population in "hearts and minds" missions. I was in a total of 6 years including two years with the national guard.

Soon after I joined the guard I was in college. I pursued a degree in philosophy, but after graduation, I was left craving adventure again. However, the army would not do. I was by this time against the idiotic miliary engagements that did nothing but waste money and lives in the service of the ugliest kinds of greed. I needed to travel. I went to California in pursuit of a job in a permacultural farm. As chance would have it, I instead began working in the cannabis industry where I spent many years doing everything from growing, to brokering marijuana, to making high quality hashish and developing techniques for making what me and my partner in that endeavor called "cold water extracts". This variety of hash contain zero solvents and is considered the healthier alternative to mass market hashish, which often contains solvents derived from petrochemicals.

The cannabis industry is fine, and today is increasing in market share, however, that's not where my heart is. I bought land right before the pandemic hit in 2019, and moved soon after the pandemic struck North America. It's a 40 acre ranch in one of the least light polluted areas in the continental United States. I named it Azura. I had no plans for it other than to transform it into a beautiful oasis. Soon I read that people were actually making money renting out areas on their land to campers. That piqued my interest and so I made the requesite calls and soon had campers on my land almost daily. The milky way is the greatest attraction however the mountains and vast xeric landscape is something truly to be beheld. Soon after all that I met my lady and we have been working to improve Azura together ever since.

Coding has been something I've always wanted to do but never could. My lady has coded before and she actually encouraged me to pursue this as a job. i tried some things here and there, learned some code and now I am steadily on a journey to master computer programing and web developement. It's been such a fun ride so far and I am excited to continue to pursue it.
